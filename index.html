Brief

I am an astronomer based in the Centre for Astrophysics Research (CAR) at the University of Hertfordshire.

My research focuses on the evolution of galaxies, with a special emphasis on obscured activity, the role of environment, and the properties and evolution of cold gas in galaxies. For a complete record of my research, you can find an up-to-date listing of publications and pre-prints at ADS.

Projects

I lead or co-lead the following projects:

  • VISTA-CFHT Stripe 82 survey (VICS82): a 180 square degree J and K(short) imaging survey of the SDSS Stripe 82 to a depth of 22 mag (AB). Our aims are to accurately measure the stellar mass function of galaxies and clusters out to z~1 in a 0.5 cubic-Gpc volume, detect intermediate redshift (and very high redshift) quasars, find distant clusters and proto-clusters of galaxies and to perform CMB-OIR cross correlation experiments. Basically contributing to the growing legacy of data in the SDSS Stripe 82 region.

  • SCUBA-2 Cluster Snapshot Survey (S2CSS): a Band 4+5 SCUBA-2 project targeting 100s of massive clusters of galaxies to hunt for the brightest (lensed) submillimetre galaxies in the sky. Ideal ALMA targets to learn about the properties of cold gas and dust in distant galaxies with incredible signal-to-noise and spatial resolution.

    • I am the survey scientist of the SCUBA-2 Cosmology Legacy Survey (S2CLS) - the largest of the JCMT Legacy Surveys. S2CLS is performing the most comprehensive survey of submillimetre galaxies ever done, potentially detecting ULIRG-like systems out to z=10. The first paper was Geach et al. (2013).
